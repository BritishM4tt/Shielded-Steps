INFORMATION:

- What is safeguarding?

Online safeguarding refers to the measures and practices put in place to protect individuals, especially children and vulnerable people, from risks and harm while using the internet. It aims to create a safe online environment by addressing potential dangers like cyberbullying, online predators, inappropriate content, scams, and privacy breaches.

- Why is safeguarding so important?

Online safeguarding is crucial because the internet, while beneficial, also exposes users to various risks such as Protection from Online Threats, Privacy and Data Security, Promotes Safe Online Behavior, and Legal and Ethical Responsibility.

Steps to Safeguarding:

Use Strong Passwords and Enable 2FA

Create strong, unique passwords for each account. Combine letters, numbers, and symbols, and avoid using easily guessed information. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for an extra layer of security.

Be Cautious with Personal Information

Limit the amount of personal information you share online. Avoid posting sensitive details like your address, phone number, or financial information on social media or unsecured websites.

Stay Aware of Scams and Cyber Threats

Be vigilant against phishing attempts, malicious links, and suspicious downloads. Regularly update your software and security settings, and use trusted antivirus programs to protect against malware.
